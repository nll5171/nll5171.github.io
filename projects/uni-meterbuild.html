Evolution of Meter Build in UNI

This video was not only an in-depth investigation into the mechanics of building meter in Under Night In-Birth, but also my attempt to learn DaVinci resolve, having recently upgraded to a computer that could handle the software. In the past I had used Adobe Premiere and After Effects, but was eager to switch to something that I wouldn't need to pay for (or be lucky enough to use through my part-time job with RIT).

Research for this video was also substantially more difficult, as while most of the game's code is still available in the high-level language it was written in (Squirrel), some of what I needed access to was stored directly in the executable rather than in separate bin files. As such, I needed to dust off my debugging skills, this time with Ghidra rather than Mame.

The researching stage took quite a bit longer than I would've hoped, as did the scriptwriting phase. I believe I began towards the start of 2025, but didn't finish the research until around March, and didn't finish the scriptwriting until around late April.

Despite learning DaVinci resolve for the first time, I was able to get the hang of things quite quickly, and, after having used it, I don't feel any need to go back to Adobe. Additionally, I was able to iterate on what I had done in the prior video on Under Night, increasing the time text was shown on-screen, and ensuring it was always properly centered, as I had been neglectful of that before.

Said process required me to learn a fair bit about fuses, and having spent time learning it, I'm confident I'd be able to accomplish far more with DaVinci resolve than I ever could with Premiere. I'm proud to have made this video, and I hope to continue making future videos in DaVinci resolve as well.
