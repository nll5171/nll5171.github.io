Haunted Hunters

Haunted Hunters was a Unity project developed in a group of 3 during my Junior year in RIT’s Game Design & Development program, and was created for the Casual Game Development class I took during the latter half of the academic year.

Since the goal was to create a game targeted towards more casual audiences, one focused on short play sessions, we decided to create an endless runner game in a similar vein to Jetpack Joyride and Subway Surfers.

Our game involves avoiding enemies and obstacles by switching between the 3 available lanes open to the player. Yellow collectibles are spawned randomly alongside obstacles and enemies that serve as ammunition that can be fired at enemies and obstacles to destroy them.

The game would progressively increase in speed the further along you went, in order to ramp up the difficulty and keep things interesting. We also included the ability to begin at a faster speed right off the bat, providing an increased score modifier based on your starting speed to compensate for the higher starting difficulty.

I was primarily responsible for the game loop, player movement, speed adjustment, scoring, and later went on to revise the system we had for spawning to adjust the percentage chances for objects spawned as the distance traveled increased, in another attempt to increase the difficulty.
