Punishment of Sisyphus

Punishment of Sisyphus was a Unity game developed in a group of 5 during my Junior year in RITâ€™s Game Design & Development program, for the Game Design & Development II class I took during the first half of the academic year.

Our project was designed to be a rogue-like featuring the character of Sisyphus from greek mythology. He would be tasked with clearing waves of enemies using his boulder while navigating up the mountain. The boulder would function akin to a pinball, ricocheting off enemies and walls, forcing the player to strategize where to launch the boulder from, and prevent it from falling offscreen, causing a game over.

Our primary inspirations for this project were the Binding of Isaac and Hades, the former for how the game would be presented and played, and the latter as inspiration for the presentation and narrative.

The current version of the game features 4 screens with 3 waves per screen of enemies, and 3 enemy types. No upgrades are available to the player, and to make the difficulty slightly easier, health is recovered between screens, though if the game were to be expanded, that likely would not be the case.

I contributed to setting up the initial and final version of the game loop, player controls, initial boulder physics as well as some of the later fine tuning, and boulder prediction, to help give the player an idea of where the boulder would end up.
