Heritage for the Future: Roman Cancel Romhack

This was a romhack of JoJo’s Bizarre Adventure: Heritage for the Future I created in the fall of 2021 prior to my start at RIT. The Roman Cancel is a technique from the Guilty Gear series of fighting games in which an individual can spend resources to cancel one of their actions and return to an actionable state. As a fan of that series, I wanted to see if I would be able to recreate that mechanic within this game.

To achieve this, I utilized a debugging tool to trace through the game’s subroutines in order to find one that was capable of returning the player to a neutral state, and the requirements for the player to be considered in one. Likewise, I also needed to find the subroutines responsible for playing sound effects and displaying visual effects on screen, in order to provide visual and auditory feedback.

With the preliminary research completed, I then proceeded to jump to empty ROM space and inject custom assembly in the routine that normally begins with the check for special move inputs. I rerouted the instruction to jump to that routine to instead go towards my custom assembly, then the special move input routine afterwards, before finally returning to where it was initially.

I ran into a few issues during this process that required some additional work. This game allows you to switch between two states at will: Stand On and Stand Off, which change the attacks you're able to perform, as well as some of your movement options. While my initial code worked for the Stand Off state, it did not work for the Stand On state. As such, I needed to write additional custom assembly to have it work for both.

The end result is a little buggy, as the game was never designed around being able to do this, but for the most part, it functions as intended. I was even able to get the romhack to have its own lobby on FightCade, a software client to play arcade titles online using rollback netcode.
