About Me

My name is Nicholas Lang, though I usually go by Nick. I'm a recent graduate of RIT's Game Design & Development program. I've loved games since I was a child and have wanted to make them for about just as long. Over time, I also became interested in analyzing how my favorite games work beneath the surface and developed a deep passion for game modding.

As someone who is primarily programming oriented, I enjoy not only game development and game modding, but software engineering as a whole. In my spare time, I also enjoy making highly edited content analyzing my favorite games in-depth. Simply put, I just enjoy making things, regardless of what it might be.

Provided below are examples of my work, both academic and personal. Most of my projects are programming ones, though I've included some of my other projects here as well. If you're at all interested in my work and what you see here, please feel free to contact me.

Academic Projects

Haunted Hunters
Spring 2024 - Casual Game Development

An endless runner group project about escaping a haunted mansion, collecting energy to dodge or destroy enemies and obstacles in your way.

I was mainly responsible for the initial concept and game loop, as well as player movement mechanics.
Punishment of Sisyphus
Fall 2023 - Game Design & Development II

A group project rogue-like centered around launching a pinball-like boulder to defeat waves of enemies.

Much of what I did centered around player input and movement, as well as some of the boulder physics and travel projection.
Vault i
Spring 2023 - Game Modding

A group project to mod Fallout New Vegas and add a brief new quest. Players investigate reports of a cryptid in the Mojave and uncover a cult.

I handled the creation of the cultists as well as their new assets (modified models from the base game). I also did some quest scripting.
Picross
Fall 2022 - Intro to Game Web Tech

A solo project done in only a few days as the final for the class. Selects a random 16x16 NES character to generate from several options.

Sound effects and music were added to improve the experience, and the grid is designed to scale in size based on screen dimensions.
Blink
Spring 2022 - Game Development & Algorithmic Problem Solving II

A puzzle game made in a group of 5 that sees the player transport crates while managing their remaining light energy to repel enemies.

I was mainly responsible for the rotation and collision of the player's light, as well as the crates.
